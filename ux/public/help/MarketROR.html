How Market Rate of Return Works

The market rate of return (default 5%) controls how fast your investment account balances grow each year. It is applied monthly to every account — savings, tax-deferred (IRA/401k), and tax-exempt (Roth).

What It Does

Each month the calculator multiplies each account balance by 1 + (annual rate ÷ 12). Over a full year this compounds to slightly more than the stated annual rate.

Annual rate Effective annual growth (compounded monthly)
0%0.00% — balances only change from deposits and withdrawals
5%5.12%
7%7.23%
10%10.47%

Why It Matters

  • Portfolio longevity — a higher rate means your savings last longer before they are depleted.
  • Offsetting shortfalls — if your expenses exceed your income, investment growth can make up part or all of the difference. For example, a $500,000 portfolio at 5% grows by roughly $25,000/year, which can offset a $15,000/year spending shortfall.
  • Roth IRA growth — tax-exempt accounts grow at this rate tax-free, making them especially valuable over a long simulation.

What It Does Not Control

The market rate of return only affects account balances. It does not affect how income or expenses grow over time — those are controlled by the inflation rate, Social Security COLA rate, or a custom rate you specify per item.

Practical Examples

Scenario Sample Chat Prompt
Use a conservative return assumption "Set the market rate of return to 4%."
Model a more optimistic portfolio "Assume a 7% annual market return."
See what happens with zero investment growth "Run the simulation with 0% market rate of return."
Key point: The market rate of return is a long-run average assumption — it does not model year-to-year volatility. Every month the calculator applies the same steady growth rate, so the results represent a smooth average-return scenario rather than a sequence-of-returns risk analysis.
