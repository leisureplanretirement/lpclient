How Inflation Works in the Retirement Calculator

The Inflation Assumptions

The calculator has three global rate assumptions. Changing any one of them affects every income or expense that uses that rate — you only need to set them once per scenario.

Rate Default What It Controls
General inflation rate 2.5% Expenses and income you want to grow with the cost of living
Medical inflation rate 4.9% Medical expenses and Medicare premiums
Social Security COLA rate 2.5% How much your Social Security benefit grows each year

How Each Income or Expense Grows Over Time

When you add an income or expense, you can tell the calculator how its amount should change each year. If you don't specify, it stays flat.

Growth type What it means
Flat Amount never changes — use this for fixed payments like a mortgage
Grows with inflation Rises each year with the general inflation rate
Grows with Social Security COLA Rises each year with the Social Security COLA rate
Grows with medical inflation Rises each year with the medical inflation rate — applied automatically to medical expenses
Custom rate Rises at a specific percentage you provide — for expenses that don't fit any global assumption

What the Calculator Does Automatically

For certain categories you don't need to specify a growth type — the calculator infers it from context.

Medical Expenses

  • Automatically grow at the medical inflation rate when you describe an expense as medical
  • You can override this by explicitly saying "keep it flat" or "grow it with general inflation"

Medicare Premiums

  • Calculated and projected automatically — you do not need to add these as separate expenses

Social Security

  • Grows with the Social Security COLA rate when you describe income as Social Security
  • You can override with "keep it flat" or a custom rate if needed

Everything Else

  • Stays flat by default — mention how you want it to grow if you want it to change over time

Practical Examples

Scenario Sample Chat Prompt
Groceries growing with inflation "Add a grocery expense of $800/month that grows with inflation."
Fixed mortgage payment "Add a mortgage payment of $2,200/month that stays flat — it never changes."
Medical out-of-pocket costs "Add a medical expense of $300/month for out-of-pocket costs."
Social Security with COLA "Add Social Security income of $2,400/month starting at age 67, growing with the Social Security COLA."
Unusual expense with its own growth rate "Add a club membership expense of $500/month that grows at 7% per year."
Change the global inflation assumption "Set the annual inflation rate to 3%."
Change the medical inflation assumption "Set the medical inflation rate to 6%."
Key point: The four rates are global — changing one affects every income and expense that uses it. For anything that doesn't fit neatly into a global assumption, just tell the calculator a specific percentage and it will use that for that item only.
