Getting Started

I'm 62 years old, married filing jointly, and planning to retire at 65. I have $400,000 in a 401(k), $150,000 in a Roth IRA, and $80,000 in savings. My wife and I expect $3,200/month from Social Security starting at 67. Our monthly expenses are about $5,500. Will our money last to age 90?
Full scenario
I'm 67 and just retired. I have $600,000 in a traditional IRA and $2,800/month in Social Security already started. I spend about $4,800/month. How long will my savings last?
Simple baseline
Can you run a basic retirement projection for a 65-year-old single filer with $500,000 total savings — half in an IRA, half in a taxable account — and Social Security of $2,200/month? Living expenses are $4,000/month.
Quick start

Accounts & Withdrawal Strategy

Withdrawal order
My taxable savings account has grown significantly — the balance is $250,000 but my cost basis is only $90,000. How does the simulator handle capital gains taxes when I withdraw from that account?
Capital gains
I expect my market return to be 4% rather than the default 5% — I'm invested mostly in bonds. How much does that lower return shorten my runway?
Rate of return
Run two scenarios side by side: one where I keep my current asset mix earning 5%, and one where I shift to a more conservative 3.5% return. Which one runs out of money first?
Scenario comparison

Itemised vs. standard

State income tax

Charitable deduction

Medical inflation

Medicare cost detail

Inflation & Spending

Inflation stress test
I expect to spend $8,000 on a home repair project in two years and another $15,000 on a car in five years. Can you add those as one-off expenses and show how they affect my balance chart?
One-off expenses

Early retirement

Longevity risk

Stress test
